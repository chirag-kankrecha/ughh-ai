## ðŸŽ“ What I Learned Building This

### 1. Content-Market Fit > Virality

**Initial approach:** Chase trending AI topics for maximum reach.

**Reality:** Evergreen relatable content outperforms viral spikes.

**What worked:** Timeless tech pain points (debugging, meetings, layoffs) that won't age.

**Pivot:** Focus on *what tech workers experience daily* vs *what's trending today*.

### 2. Shareability = Product Design

**Discovery:** Users share content that helps them express feelings they can't articulate at work.

**Example:** "CEO says we're family" â†’ "Mass layoff Friday" hits hard because it's *specific and true*.

**Product insight:** Memes are emotional labor outsourcing. Make it easy for people to say the hard thing.

### 3. No Hashtags = Quality Filter

**Experiment:** Posted with zero hashtags from day one.

**Result:** Every follower came from shares, not discovery.

**Learning:** Forced me to make content so relatable that people *have* to send it to their coworkers.

**Trade-off:** Slower growth, but higher engagement and better audience fit.

### 4. Telegram Control = Speed + Quality

**Challenge:** Need fast iteration but also quality control.

**Solution:** Telegram bot generates drafts â†’ I review â†’ approve/reject.

**Benefit:** Can generate 20 memes in 10 minutes, but only post the ones that land.
